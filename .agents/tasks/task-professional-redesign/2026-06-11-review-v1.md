# Professional redesign: homepage, products, about, and content cleanup

Deekshi Global Exim's website has been restructured to present the company as a pan-India agricultural exporter rather than a regional AP/Telangana sourcing operation. The homepage lost three sections (PriorityExportProducts, TradeProcess, Testimonials), gained a cleaner section ordering, and every remaining component was rewritten to remove verbose badge-heavy copy in favor of concise professional descriptions. The products page was rebuilt with 5 categories (adding Dehydrated Products) and stripped of per-product state-sourcing badges. About and Certifications pages were scrubbed of geographic specificity while retaining the physical address in the footer.

**Watch for:** Dead component file left in the codebase (PriorityExportProducts.tsx still exists, now returns `null`) [confirmed]. Dehydrated Products category image reuses the turmeric photo from Spices, which could confuse buyers [confirmed]. Two homepage sections removed (TradeProcess, Testimonials) without their component files being deleted — they still exist in the source tree as unused code [confirmed].

## High-level view

The homepage section composition was simplified from 9 sections to 7, removing the priority export showcase (state-specific by nature), the trade process (duplicated on the certifications page), and testimonials. The remaining sections were reordered to put FeaturedProducts and CertificationsPreview before WhyChooseUs, creating a flow of "what we sell → why trust us → why choose us → CTA." Removing testimonials leaves no social proof mechanism on the homepage.

The products page moved from long-form descriptions with state-sourcing badges per product to short, professional descriptions focused on product characteristics and export readiness. A new Dehydrated Products category with Garlic Powder and Onion Powder was added as the fifth category. The shortened descriptions achieve the cleanliness goal but lose concrete technical details (GI values, capacity figures, variety specifics) that international buyers use for procurement decisions.

Content across About, Certifications, and Footer consistently replaced "Andhra Pradesh and Telangana" references with "India" or "Indian," and updated product lists to include the new dehydrated category.

## Homepage section composition and dead code

The homepage previously ran: Hero → About → Stats → PriorityExports → WhyChooseUs → FeaturedProducts → Certifications → TradeProcess → Testimonials → CTA. The new ordering is: Hero → About → Stats → FeaturedProducts → Certifications → WhyChooseUs → CTA.

Three component files remain in the source tree as dead code:
- `PriorityExportProducts.tsx` — gutted to `export default function PriorityExportProducts() { return null }` rather than deleted.
- `TradeProcess.tsx` — full implementation still present, no longer imported anywhere.
- `Testimonials.tsx` — full implementation still present, no longer imported anywhere.

All three should be deleted. The `PriorityExportProducts` approach of returning null is particularly confusing — it suggests the component might be conditionally rendered somewhere, when in fact it's simply abandoned.

The removal of testimonials also means the homepage has no social proof element. This may or may not matter depending on the target audience, but it's worth noting as a content gap.

## Products page: image collision and content trade-off

The products page added Dehydrated Products as a fifth category with Garlic Powder and Onion Powder. The category-level preview image on the homepage FeaturedProducts component uses `photo-1615485500704-8e990f9900f7` — the same turmeric photo that represents the Turmeric product in the Spices section of the products page. A buyer clicking "Dehydrated Products" sees a turmeric photo and lands on garlic/onion products. This should be a distinct image showing dehydrated garlic or onion powder.

The product descriptions were shortened aggressively. Sona Masoori went from mentioning "fertile river basins," "low glycemic index," "5kg to 50MT bulk shipments with custom branding options" to "Premium medium-grain rice known for its lightweight texture and excellent cooking quality." The state-specific sourcing language is correctly removed, but technical details like GI values, shipment capacity ranges, and variety sub-types (1121, PUSA, Traditional for Basmati) were also stripped. These aren't regional claims — they're product specifications that matter to importers. A middle ground would add one technical differentiator per product while keeping descriptions to 2 sentences.

## WhyChooseUs reduction

The section went from 6 value propositions in a 3-column grid to 4 in a 4-column grid. The removed cards — "Competitive Direct-Farm Pricing" and "Multi-Market Experience" — contained the most differentiated messaging (bypassing middlemen, market-specific regulatory knowledge). What remains is generic enough to apply to any exporter. If the goal is to match competitors like Bharani Exim, the cards should still communicate what makes Deekshi different, not just what it does.

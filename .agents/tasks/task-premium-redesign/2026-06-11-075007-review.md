# Rice-first premium redesign for Deekshi Global Exim

This change repositions the entire site around rice as the flagship export product. Every customer-facing section — hero, products, about, certifications, CTAs, testimonials — has been rewritten to lead with Sona Masoori and Basmati rice, reference AP/Telangana sourcing, and address international bulk buyers directly. A new `PriorityExportProducts` section was added to the homepage, and the products page gained a full Rice category at the top. The certifications and about pages now frame every credential and capability in terms of buyer protection and supply reliability.

**Watch for:** A malformed Unsplash image URL for the Potato product will produce a broken image in production (confirmed). Multiple product pairs share identical images, creating a visual credibility problem for an export-facing site (confirmed). All four CTAs in the CtaBanner route to the same `/contact` page despite promising distinct actions like "Download Catalog" (confirmed).

## High-level view

A new `PriorityExportProducts` component gives the homepage an explicit priority-ordered product showcase (rice at #1). It coexists with the rewritten `FeaturedProducts` section immediately below, meaning the homepage now has two product grid sections in near-sequence — one with priority badges and one with category badges — which creates visual redundancy.

Image handling has gaps. One Unsplash URL is clearly malformed and will 404. Several products (Non-Basmati Rice, Garlic Powder, Cumin, Cardamom) reuse the same photo as a different product on the same page, undermining the premium positioning the copy works hard to establish.

The four-CTA grid in `CtaBanner` is visually strong but functionally hollow: all four buttons link to `/contact` with no query parameters, pre-filled subjects, or distinct routing. A buyer clicking "Download Catalog" lands on the same form as "Request Quotation."

Metrics like "500+ Tons/Month" and "15+ Countries" appear as hardcoded strings in at least three separate files with no shared constant. The homepage `metadata.description` was not updated to match the rice-first positioning applied everywhere else — an SEO gap on the most-indexed page.

## Malformed and duplicate image URLs

The Potato product in `src/app/products/page.tsx` uses `photo-1518977676601-b53f82ber40` — the trailing `ber40` is not a valid hex segment for an Unsplash photo ID (they end in hex characters 0-9, a-f). This will return a 404 from Unsplash and render as a broken image against the gold gradient placeholder.

Beyond the broken URL, several products share images with unrelated products on the same page:

```
Non-Basmati Rice  → same image as Sona Masoori Rice (photo-1586201375761...)
Garlic Powder     → same image as Pomegranate (photo-1615485290382...)
Cumin             → same image as Black Pepper (photo-1599909533601...)
Cardamom          → same image as Coriander (photo-1596040033229...)
```

For a site positioning itself as premium and trustworthy to international commodity buyers, reusing a pomegranate photo for garlic powder is a credibility risk. Each product should have its own image, even if it means a generic "spice powder" or "seeds" photo rather than a mismatched one.

## CTA grid promising distinct actions, delivering one destination

The `CtaBanner` redesign presents four distinct buyer actions in a 2x2 grid. But all four `href` values are `/contact` with no differentiator. The "Request Product Catalog" CTA's description says "Download our full export product catalog" — implying a download action — but delivers a generic contact form.

Options to make this meaningful: append query parameters (`/contact?intent=catalog`), use hash anchors, or implement a lightweight PDF download for the catalog button. Without differentiation, the four-button layout trains buyers to distrust the specificity of CTAs elsewhere on the site.

## Two product showcases in sequence on the homepage

The homepage renders `PriorityExportProducts` followed by `WhyChooseUs` followed by `FeaturedProducts`. Both product sections display rice, mangoes/fruits, onions, chillies, and turmeric in card grids with Unsplash images, badges, and "Request Quote" / "Enquire Now" CTAs. The only distinction is priority numbering (#1-#6) vs. category labels.

```
Homepage section order:
  HeroSection
  AboutPreview
  StatsSection
  PriorityExportProducts  ← 6 product cards with priority badges
  WhyChooseUs
  FeaturedProducts         ← 6 product cards with category badges
  CertificationsPreview
  TradeProcess
  Testimonials
  CtaBanner
```

A visitor scrolling through sees two near-identical product grids separated by one section. Consider removing `FeaturedProducts` from the homepage and keeping it only on the `/products` page, or differentiating the two sections more sharply (e.g., make `FeaturedProducts` a compact 3-card highlight rather than a full 6-card grid).

## Stat consistency across components

The "500+ Tons Monthly Capacity" claim appears in:
- `StatsSection.tsx` (as stat card)
- `src/app/about/page.tsx` (as info card and floating badge)
- `WhyChooseUs.tsx` (prose description of FCL/LCL capabilities)

Similarly "15+ Countries" appears in `StatsSection` and `WhyChooseUs`. These are hardcoded strings with no shared source of truth. When the real business metrics change, one component will get updated and others won't, leaving inconsistent claims visible to the same buyer in one session.

## Homepage metadata drift

`src/app/page.tsx` metadata description still reads:

> "Premium exporter of fruits, vegetables, rice & spices from India."

Every other page's metadata was updated to lead with rice. This is the page Google indexes most heavily, and it doesn't reflect the rice-first positioning.
